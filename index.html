HERITAGE
Chapter One · Fall 1959

A Club Born
at a Party.

"I can form a rugby team that could beat any other in the province of Alberta."

— Chris Battin, founder, a Welshman with a point to prove

That boast at a party in 1959 became a rugby club. A Welshman, Chris Battin, made the declaration and followed through. His father Tom Battin became the team's first trainer. And the name — The Druids — came from first captain Brian Jones, who chose it for a club that was already wearing red.

The early years were quiet. Then the roof came off. From 1966 to 1968, The Druids took three consecutive Edmonton and Alberta championship titles. Titles would keep coming at the city and provincial level, and the club would go on to test itself against international opposition around the world.

Through the 1980s the club opened its doors to junior boys. The 1990s added junior girls and senior women's rugby. In 1997, the Edmonton Druids merged with the Strathcona Outlaws — a union that brought the club home to a proper ground in Sherwood Park. Sixty-six years on, the Druids remain a mainstay of rugby in Strathcona County.

The Ledger

Sixty-Six
Seasons.

From one Welshman's declaration to a $699K grant. Every chapter earned.

  1. 1959 Founded

    Chris Battin's challenge at a party becomes a club. Brian Jones is first captain and names it The Druids. Tom Battin trains the squad.

  2. 1966–68 Three In A Row

    Three consecutive Edmonton and Alberta championship titles. The club finds its edge, and the travel starts.

  3. 1975 Druid Security

    The club's own security group goes pro — later running events at Commonwealth Stadium, Rexall Place and the Grey Cup.

  4. 1980s Junior Boys Open

    The Druids expand from a senior men's club to a pipeline, launching a junior boys program that feeds every side after.

  5. 1990s Girls + Women + Merger

    Junior girls and senior women's programs join the club. In 1997 the Edmonton Druids merge with the Strathcona Outlaws — and the Druids come home to Sherwood Park.

  6. 2010 50th Anniversary

    The grounds are renamed Lynn Davies Rugby Park, honouring the man who acquired and maintained them, and brought rugby to Strathcona County.

  7. 2020 60th Anniversary

    Six decades of rugby in the Edmonton and Strathcona communities.

  8. 2026 The Next Chapter

    A $699,508 provincial grant lands. A new modular clubhouse and realigned pitches are on the way. Built with Stalcorp, Total Modular Limited, and McElhanney.

From U5 flag on Saturday mornings to senior men's and women's sides every weekend. Qualified coaches. Game-day jerseys provided. Friendships that outlast the bruises.

Flag rugby for U5–U9 (non-contact); contact rugby from U11. Saturday morning festivals May–July. Flag belts and rugby balls provided; red shirts are standard kit for the youngest age groups.
